1. Yes, Lance is a columnar format. It's also a table format, similar to Iceberg and Delta Lake. 2. (a) I do not understand what you mean by "default worst-case scenario". Is there a specific workload you are interested in? 2. (b) Are you taking about KNN/ANN search here? If so, lance.search() would give k number of rows back, and you could pass that to Polars if you wanted to. 3. If your workload involves many write queries affecting random rows, you likely want an OLTP database, not a OLAP one. I'd recomend looking up those terms if you aren't familiar, as they will be helpful in guiding your towards an appropriate storage technology. 4. What "an efficient mutable arrow format" means will really depend on your workload. I'd think about how many QPS (queries per second) you expect for the workloads: (1) Bulk/OLAP scan, (2) ANN/KNN search or random single-row reads, (3) single row writes, (4) bulk writes. Lance is good with (1), (2), (4), not as much with (3).

To be clear, in both Delta lake/Iceberg and Lance, the data files (Parquet or Lance) are immutable.
