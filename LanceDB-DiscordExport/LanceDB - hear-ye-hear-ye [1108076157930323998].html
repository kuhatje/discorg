At Uber scale, a single object store becomes a bottleneck. For large AI training and search workloads, Uber‚Äôs AI infrastructure team needed one Lance dataset to span multiple S3 buckets so reads and writes could fan out in parallel. Most table formats struggle here:
  • Absolute paths make relocation expensive since every metadata entry must be rewritten
  • Relative paths preserve portability, but assume all data lives under one root Lance‚Äôs answer is a multi-base layout. Instead of storing full paths per file, tables declare a small set of storage bases, and files reference them by ID. That lets one dataset scale across buckets while keeping metadata small and relocatable. This design came directly from Uber‚Äôs production requirements, and it generalizes to multi-region layouts, tiered storage, and shallow clones. Huge thanks to Jay Narale and the Uber AI Infrastructure team for pushing this into production and helping validate it at scale.
